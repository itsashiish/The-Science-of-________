Research Synthesis

The Science of Swearing

An interactive analysis of how profane language affects pain tolerance, social bonding, and cognitive perception. Based on research from Keele University, Marist College, and sociological studies.

Primary Sources Analyzed

  • Stephens, R. et al. (Keele University) - Hypoalgesic effects of swearing
  • Jay, T. & Jay, K. (Marist College) - Taboo word fluency and general verbal fluency
  • Feldman, G. et al. - Profanity as a self-correction mechanism and honesty metric
  • Byrne, R. - Social bonding in workplace cultures

About This Interface

This Single Page Application synthesizes quantitative and qualitative data to explore the nuance of language. It demonstrates that swearing is not merely "bad habit" but a complex physiological and sociological tool. No external graphics or SVG files were used in the creation of this interface.
